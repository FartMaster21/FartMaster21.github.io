Brief Explanation of Vocaloid
Vocaloid is a voice synthesizer software that can be used in songs for vocals. Vocaloid works kind of like an instrument and can be tuned to sound differently, depending on the producer utilizing the software or the tone of the song. Vocaloid was originally created by Yamaha and has been around since the 2000's. This software's vocals come from real voice providers, it is not AI. However, there are modern synthesizers like SynthV that utilize AI, but they do not utilize generative AI. Anwyay, there are lots of Vocaloids, meaning that there are many different voice synthesizers with different voice providers. The characters you see associated with Vocaloids, like Miku, are bascially mascots for the synthesizing software, originally created to sell the software. In this EP, the Vocaloids Hatsune Miku and Megurine Luka were used.

1. Megurin Q (EP Ver.) (feat. Megurine Luka)

This song is a very strong start to the EP. The bass at the start is very groovy, it makes you want to bop your head. I think that Kiichi (the artist/producer) does a great job with Luka's tuning and the instrumentals. Some could say that the music is reminiscent of video game soundtracks and I'd say I kind of agree. Though there are other songs on this EP that encapsulate that vibe better in my opinion. This track has quite a dramatic sound to it in my opinion. The piano adds to the dramatic flair of it. At about the middle of the track, the song kind of slows down and sounds almost a bit sinister, before coming back up again. Finally, that bass once again...

2. Sishunki Of The Dead

This song is more suspenseful sounding and less upbeat. The intro and background instrumental sounds almost like a heartbeat. The vocals are more light sounding and I think this may be attributed to this song being sung by Hatsune Miku instead of Megurine Luka. Though Kiichi may have tuned it so her voice sounded more light and higher. I love this track. It's so beautiful. I like the part at 1:40 where the vocals kind of stop and the instrumental keeps going. Almost as if something were about to happen. And then the bass starts, reminiscent of the bass in the previous track. Then it takes a more serious turn, almost kind of dark. I wouldn't say menancing, but something along those lines. Around 3:22 the instrumental switches a bit, it's a subtle change. Towards the end there are these electric sounds that I love. This is one of my favorite songs on the EP.

3. Fluticasone (feat. Megurine Luka)

At this point, the EP gets fast-paced again. Kiichi does an awesome job at using unique and experimental sounds and I think this track is a great example of that. This track is unmistakably unique. Luka's vocals work great for this song, as they're stronger then Miku's. At the very least, they are tuned to be stronger in this. Luka's vocals at 1:05 are a stark contrast to the previous minute of the song and introduce a kind of switch in the song. The instrumentals and vocals work together to create such a fun and head-bopping track. At around 2:00, the beat changes and Luka's vocals take control again. Great use of electronic beats and sounds in this track.

4. Virgo (feat. Megurine Luka)

This songs continues the fast-paced vibe from the previous song. I love this track. It's so robotic and electronic sounding. Another great use of Megurine Luka's voice. I could bop my head to this whole EP, but this track especially. The part at 0:52 is so good. The slighlty unsettling piano and change in instrumentals. I could see someone voguing to this part. At 1:09, Luka starts to hold longer notes and kind of belt (I don't really know singing terms). I feel like this song might be a bit unsettling to some people, but the people that get it, get it. I just love that piano and the beat change that follows it. And Luka's vocals are so unique. Apparently, the lyrics to this song are all gibberish. How interesting!

5. Inquisitor Girl Sister Kanon (feat. Megurine Luka)

Oh I love love love this song. That intro is just so good. It's evil and dramatic like a boss battle. Luka is also tuned in a way where her voice sounds deeper and I think it suits the tone of the track a lot. The sounds are just so unique I love it. At 0:34, the vocals start almost building up. Then at around 1:00, the vocals get kind of different, I'm not sure how to explain it. Like they're moving up and down and up and down, as well as being kind layered. But as the song continues there's a part at like 1:53 where the song changes a bit, like it's keying up for something... and then the previous pattern of the song continues. At the end kind of around 3:14, there's this beat in the background that's cool, it kind of sounds like a piano, but kinda different... not sure. Sick nonetheless.

6. Banshee Strikes (feat. Hatsune Miku)

Oh boy. The intro to this track is just too good. Video game-esque. I think using Miku was a great choice for this one as her vocals don't overwhelm the instrumental. This track has a lot of screams and long notes by Miku. There's also this grating sound at the start of and throughout the song that I think adds so much to the dramatic, rushed, kind of scary vibe of the song. At 2:00, there's a return to the instrumental that hits so hard, followed by that grating sound again. Like a quick break from the song. At 3:20 there's this funny sound that sounds like someone getting hit in the head in a cartoon multiple times. If you're not a big fan of loud and dramatic vocals, this one (especially) might not be for you.

7. (development)

This song is the closer of the EP and is purely intsrumental. It's very ominous sounding and doesn't have much going on, but it's not really supposed to. Like most intro or outro songs on an album/EP it's just there to kind of end or start the EP. I think it's cool though!

Final Rating: 8/10

I love this EP so much. A great example of a vocaloid producer experimenting with noises and electronica. I couldn't pinpoint a single genre for this album as I think it falls within various genres. Kiichi does a bit of everything in this EP in my opinion. I've seen it been described as electropop, among various other genres. Anyway, I do think this EP in particular makes great use of electronic sounds and unique vocals and instrumentals. It keeps you on your feet and is quite experimental (at least in my opinion). This EP and producer (Kiichi) is on my list of recommendations when it comes to Vocaloid and electronica.
